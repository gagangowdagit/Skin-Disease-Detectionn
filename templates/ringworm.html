Ringworm - Treatment and Recommendations

Note: First preference is to consult a skin doctor.

1. Prescription Treatments for Ringworm

  • Topical Antifungal Creams (e.g., Clotrimazole, Miconazole, Terbinafine): These are commonly prescribed for mild to moderate ringworm infections. Apply to the affected area as directed until the infection clears.
  • Oral Antifungal Medications (e.g., Terbinafine, Griseofulvin, Itraconazole): For severe or widespread infections, oral antifungals may be prescribed to ensure the infection is fully treated.
  • Topical Steroid-Antifungal Combinations: In some cases, a combination of antifungal treatment and a mild steroid may be prescribed to reduce inflammation associated with the infection.
  • Antifungal Shampoos (e.g., Ketoconazole, Selenium Sulfide): For scalp ringworm (tinea capitis), antifungal shampoos can be used to control the infection.

2. Diet Recommendations

Foods to Include:

  • Probiotic-Rich Foods: Foods like yogurt, kefir, and sauerkraut can help improve gut health and boost the immune system, which can aid in fighting fungal infections.
  • Garlic: Known for its antifungal and immune-boosting properties, garlic can help reduce fungal infections.
  • Zinc-Rich Foods: Zinc supports the immune system and skin healing. Foods like pumpkin seeds, beans, and lentils are good sources.
  • Vitamin C-Rich Foods: Vitamin C boosts immunity and supports skin health. Include citrus fruits like oranges, strawberries, and bell peppers.

Foods to Avoid:

  • Sugary Foods: Fungal infections can thrive on sugar. Avoid candy, soda, and processed foods high in refined sugar during the treatment.
  • Dairy Products: Some individuals find that dairy products can promote fungal growth. You may want to limit or avoid them during the infection.
  • Alcohol: Alcohol can weaken the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight the fungal infection.

3. Clean Procedures and Hygiene

  • Clean the Area Gently: Wash the infected area with soap and water, and pat it dry. Avoid scrubbing the infection as it can irritate the skin.
  • Use Antifungal Powders or Creams: Apply antifungal treatments as directed by your doctor, usually after cleaning and drying the affected area.
  • Avoid Scratching: Ringworm can be itchy, but scratching can spread the infection to other parts of the body or others. Keep the area clean and use antifungal cream regularly.
  • Wash Clothes and Bedding Frequently: Fungus can survive on fabrics, so wash clothes, towels, and bedding regularly in hot water to prevent reinfection.
  • Keep the Area Dry: Fungi thrive in warm, moist environments. Keep the affected area dry and avoid tight clothing that may cause sweating.

4. Lifestyle Modifications

  • Practice Good Hygiene: Wash your hands regularly, especially after touching the infected area, to prevent spreading the fungus.
  • Avoid Sharing Personal Items: Do not share towels, clothes, or hairbrushes to prevent spreading the infection to others.
  • Keep Skin Dry: Since fungi thrive in moist environments, wear breathable clothing and avoid tight shoes or socks that can trap moisture.
  • Boost Your Immune System: Eat a balanced diet, get regular exercise, and manage stress to help strengthen your immune system and fight the infection more effectively.
  • Avoid Contact with Infected Individuals or Animals: Ringworm can spread easily through direct contact. Avoid contact with others who have the infection and be cautious around animals that may carry it.
