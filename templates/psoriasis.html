Psoriasis - Treatment and Recommendations

Note: First preference is to consult a skin doctor.

1. Prescription Treatments for Psoriasis

  • Topical Corticosteroids: These are the most common treatments for psoriasis flare-ups. Examples include hydrocortisone and betamethasone, which help reduce inflammation and control symptoms.
  • Topical Vitamin D Analogues (e.g., Calcipotriene, Calcitriol): These help slow down the rapid growth of skin cells and reduce inflammation.
  • Topical Retinoids (e.g., Tazarotene): These are used to reduce the scaling of the skin by normalizing cell production.
  • Coal Tar: This can help reduce inflammation, scaling, and itchiness. It is often used in combination with other treatments.
  • Phototherapy (UVB Light Therapy): Exposure to ultraviolet (UV) light under medical supervision helps slow the growth of skin cells and can alleviate symptoms in moderate to severe cases.
  • Systemic Treatments (Oral or Injectable): These are prescribed for moderate to severe psoriasis and include:
    • Methotrexate: A drug that suppresses the immune system to reduce inflammation.
    • Acitretin: A systemic retinoid that helps slow skin cell growth.
    • Biologic Medications (e.g., Adalimumab, Etanercept): These target specific parts of the immune system involved in the inflammatory process and are usually administered via injection or infusion.

2. Diet Recommendations

* Foods to Include:

  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Found in fatty fish (salmon, mackerel), walnuts, and flaxseeds, omega-3s have anti-inflammatory properties that can help manage psoriasis symptoms.
  • Antioxidant-Rich Foods: Fruits and vegetables (e.g., berries, spinach, kale) that are rich in antioxidants help reduce inflammation and oxidative stress, supporting overall skin health.
  • Vitamin D-Rich Foods: Vitamin D plays a role in skin health. Include fortified foods (e.g., milk, cereal), eggs, and fatty fish to maintain adequate vitamin D levels.
  • Whole Grains: Foods like oats, quinoa, and brown rice help reduce inflammation and support overall health.
  • Probiotics: Yogurt, kefir, and fermented foods can improve gut health, which may reduce inflammation in the body and help manage psoriasis.

  • Processed Foods: Highly processed and junk foods can trigger inflammation in the body, leading to worsened psoriasis symptoms.
  • Alcohol: Excessive alcohol consumption may trigger flare-ups and interfere with psoriasis treatments.
  • Dairy Products: Some people with psoriasis find that dairy products worsen their symptoms. Avoiding milk, cheese, and other dairy products may be beneficial.
  • Sugar and Refined Carbs: Foods like candy, pastries, and white bread can increase inflammation and trigger flare-ups.

3. Clean Procedures and Hygiene

Common Care and Hygiene Tips:

  • Moisturize Regularly: Keeping the skin hydrated is essential for managing psoriasis. Use thick, fragrance-free creams and ointments immediately after bathing to lock in moisture.
  • Gentle Cleansing: Use mild, non-soap cleansers and lukewarm water when washing. Avoid hot water, as it can dry out and irritate the skin.
  • Avoid Scratching: Scratching can lead to skin damage and increase the risk of infection. Try to control the urge by applying topical treatments or a cold compress.
  • Avoid Irritating Fabrics: Wear soft, breathable fabrics like cotton to avoid friction and irritation that can worsen psoriasis lesions.
  • Use Fragrance-Free Products: Choose fragrance-free lotions, soaps, and laundry detergents to prevent skin irritation.

4. Lifestyle Modifications

  • Manage Stress: Stress is a common trigger for psoriasis flare-ups. Practices like yoga, meditation, and deep breathing exercises can help reduce stress levels.
  • Regular Exercise: Exercise can help reduce inflammation and improve circulation, contributing to overall skin health.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drinking plenty of water helps keep your skin hydrated and supports overall health.
  • Protect Your Skin from Injury: Avoid cuts, scrapes, or sunburns, as injuries to the skin can trigger psoriasis flare-ups.
  • Get Enough Sleep: Sleep is important for overall health and immune function. Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night to support skin healing and manage symptoms.
