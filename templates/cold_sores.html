Cold Sores - Treatment and Recommendations

Note: First preference is to consult a skin doctor.

1.Prescription Treatments:

  • Antiviral Creams (e.g., Acyclovir, Docosanol): Applied directly to the affected area, these creams help reduce the severity and duration of the cold sore outbreak.
  • Oral Antiviral Medications (e.g., Acyclovir, Valacyclovir, Famciclovir): Taken orally, these medications can shorten the duration of an outbreak and prevent recurrent outbreaks if taken early.
  • Over-the-counter Topical Treatments: Some creams containing numbing agents like lidocaine can help reduce pain or discomfort during an outbreak.

2. Diet Recommendations:

    * Foods to Include:
  • Lysine-Rich Foods: Lysine can help reduce the frequency of cold sore outbreaks. Foods like fish, chicken, dairy, and beans are high in lysine.
  • Vitamin C-Rich Foods: Helps support the immune system. Include fruits like oranges, strawberries, and kiwi.
  • Garlic: Known for its antiviral properties, garlic can help strengthen the immune system.
  • Zinc-Rich Foods: Zinc supports the immune system. Foods like pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, and lentils are great sources.

    • * Foods to Include:
  • Arginine-Rich Foods: Arginine can trigger cold sores in some people. Avoid foods like chocolate, nuts, and seeds during an outbreak.
  • High-Sugar Foods: Excess sugar can weaken the immune system and may trigger outbreaks. Limit sugary snacks, sodas, and processed foods.
  • Acidic Foods: Citrus fruits and spicy foods can irritate the sores and worsen the discomfort during an outbreak.

3. Clean Procedures and Hygiene

  • Clean the Area Gently: Use a mild soap and water to clean the affected area without irritating the sore.
  • Avoid Touching the Cold Sore: Cold sores are contagious. Try not to touch them, and if you do, wash your hands immediately.
  • Use a Lip Balm or Ointment: To prevent cracking and chapping, use a lip balm with sunscreen to keep the affected area moisturized.

  • Wash Your Hands Frequently: This reduces the chance of spreading the virus to other areas of your body or to other people.

4. Lifestyle Modifications

  • Stay Hydrated: Drinking plenty of water can help keep your body hydrated, supporting your immune system.
  • Manage Stress: Stress can trigger cold sore outbreaks. Practice relaxation techniques such as meditation, yoga, or breathing exercises.
  • Get Enough Sleep: Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep per night to help your immune system function properly.
  • Boost Your Immune System: Regular exercise and a balanced diet can strengthen your immune system, potentially reducing the frequency of cold sores.
